Broken Homes & Broken Hearts: Offering New Hope to Children of Divorce

Michael Coggin

The impact of divorce on children, the family, the church, and the greater culture has been grossly underestimated. The cultural message communicated to today’s children and families is that divorce isn’t a big deal. In reality divorce is a life-transforming experience that is a watershed moment in the heart of a child. How are we as parents, youth leaders, and counselors to come alongside these children? What hope for healing is there for families dealing with this loss?

Flatter Abs, Bigger Biceps, Lonely Hearts

Travis Stewart

Body Image
Everywhere we look we see perfect bodies…and we feel like we got the short end of the stick. This is even more true for our children who are more exposed to the media as well as going through the changes of puberty and growth. These struggles with body image can lead to a host of problems, for boys and girls, including laxative and steroid abuse, depression, self-harm and eating disorders. This seminar will cover the biblical understanding of the body, media distortion, underlying issues and practical tips for equipping your children (and yourself) in this vital area of health.

The Family Table

Travis Stewart

Relating as a family to help protect your kids from eating disorders and other destructive behaviors.
Alcohol abuse, drug use, pre-marital sexual activity and Eating Disorders. None of these (or other dangerous behaviors) develop in a vacuum. Peer pressure, media, and trauma all can have an impact on whether or not our kids will engage in these behaviors. But, perhaps nothing will influence their behaviors more than the family setting. Travis, who daily works with adolescents and their families facing eating disorders (as well as many of these other behaviors), will share insights into how the family system impacts and is affected by these behaviors. Not limited to Eating Disorders alone – but specifically geared toward them – this workshop will help you understand what your child’s behaviors may have to do with how you live family life and how to love them to health.

Why Don’t You Just Eat?!

Travis Stewart

When and How to get Help for Someone with an Eating Disorder
Travis works with parents who are at the end of their rope. They’ve brought their daughter to a treatment center for Anorexia and Bulimia in the middle of the desert in Arizona in an attempt to save their lives. “Why don’t you just eat?!” is just one of the many approaches they’ve tried in getting their daughters to health…to no avail. Is there anything that can be done to help short of sending your child off to treatment? Are their signs of an eating disorder that can help you spot trouble early? When should you seek professional help? Is there really anything you can do? This seminar will help you understand the complex nature of Eating Disorders and what steps to take to get someone the help they need.
